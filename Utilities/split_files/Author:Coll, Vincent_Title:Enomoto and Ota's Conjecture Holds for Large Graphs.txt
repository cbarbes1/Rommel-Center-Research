TI Enomoto and Ota's Conjecture Holds for Large Graphs
SO GRAPHS AND COMBINATORICS
LA English
DT Article
DE Path partition; Regularity lemma; Degree sum
AB In 2000, Enomoto and Ota conjectured that if a graph G satisfies sigma(2) (G) >= |G| + k-1, then for any set of k vertices nu(1),..., nu(k) and for any positive integers n(1),..., n(k) with Sigma n(i) = |G|, there exists a partition of V (G) into k paths P-1,..., P-k such that v(i) is an end of P-i and |P-i| = n(i) for all i. We prove this conjecture when |G| is large. Our proof uses the Regularity Lemma along with several extremal lemmas, concluding with an absorbing argument to retrieve misbehaving vertices.
